* EMT Boundary Hook Design

** Decision

EMT uses Emacs' =find-word-boundary-function-table= for Han characters and
delegates segmentation to the Rust =jieba-rs= module.

** Why this approach

- The old implementation depended on Emacs' built-in word motion to discover
  text ranges, which is the wrong layer for fixing Chinese word boundaries.
- Word-boundary hooks let EMT fix the boundary layer directly, so built-in
  commands that use =forward-word= and =backward-word= improve automatically.
- Buffer-local caches keyed by Han runs and invalidated by
  =buffer-chars-modified-tick= are simpler and more correct than keeping a
  global LRU of stale strings.

** Deliberate limitations

- EMT is Han-focused.  It does not segment kana or hangul.
- Two private compatibility shims remain in =emt.el= for =meow-cjk=.  They are
  retained intentionally even though the general project guideline prefers
  deleting compatibility layers.
